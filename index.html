Coconut-Lime Rum

    Steps

    1. Mix and taste. Combine all liquids in a pitcher matched to your scaled volume. Stir well. Taste — tropical, slightly tart, sweeter than your target finished drink. Malibu is already sweet, go easy on adjustments.
    2. Chill the base. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes before loading — Malibu and pineapple juice both benefit from starting cold.
    3. Load the machine. Pour to your machine's max fill line. Lock the bail handle, confirm the seal.
    4. Select SPIKED SLUSH. Lower ABV than tequila builds means this may freeze faster — check around the 30-minute mark.
    5. Run cycle. Typical cycle 15–45 min. If texture runs thick, press DOWN to drop temperature.
    6. Serve. Garnish with lime or pineapple. Salted or Tajín rim works well here.

    Notes

    • Why this ratio works: Malibu at 21% ABV contributes far less alcohol per ounce than tequila, so the rum fraction can stay generous without pushing past the safe window.
    • Sugar floor: pineapple juice clears the 4% minimum easily — you're unlikely to need extra simple syrup.
    • Variation: swap lime for passion fruit juice for a more tropical profile, or add 2 oz coconut cream per 72 oz (reduce water 2 oz) for a piña colada direction.
    ⚠ Scaling preserves ABV automatically — ratios are fixed, not the slider. Always re-taste after rescaling.

    Ingredients

      Steps

      1. Mix and taste. Combine both rums, passion fruit juice, orange juice, lime juice, grenadine, simple syrup, and water in a pitcher. Stir well. Taste — tart-tropical with real depth from the dark rum, sweet but not cloying.
      2. Chill the base. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes before loading.
      3. Load the machine. Pour to your scaled max fill line. Lock the bail handle, confirm the seal.
      4. Select SPIKED SLUSH. At ~11% ABV this sits mid-range in the safe window — check around the 30-minute mark since exact freeze time depends on your machine and starting temperature.
      5. Run cycle. Typical cycle 15–45 min. If texture runs thick, press DOWN to drop temperature.
      6. Serve. Garnish with an orange slice and a cherry — the traditional hurricane look.

      Notes

      • Why 20 oz total rum works: at 40% ABV combined, 20 oz in 72 oz total lands at 11.1% — clear of both the 2.8% floor and 16% ceiling, with headroom to push stronger.
      • Sugar floor: grenadine and simple syrup clear the 4% minimum easily on top of natural juice sugars.
      • Dark rum matters: don't substitute a second light rum — dark rum (e.g. Myers's Dark) gives the actual flavor signature and color, not just the grenadine.
      • Variation: shift 4 oz from orange juice to passion fruit juice for a more passion-fruit-forward profile — same total volume and ABV.
      ⚠ Two-rum split (50/50 light/dark) scales together as one ratio — don't adjust independently or you'll shift both flavor and ABV math.

      Margarita — Water + Simple Syrup

      Classic tequila build. Simple syrup is a 1:1 volume substitute for agave — dissolves easily cold. SPIKED SLUSH preset.

      72 oz
      11.1%
      Final ABV — safe window 2.8%–16%
      within range

      Drunk-O-Meter™

      Basically JuiceSpicySend Help
      🧃

      Ingredients

        Steps

        1. Mix and taste. Combine all liquids in a large pitcher. Stir well — simple syrup dissolves easily cold. Taste before loading: should be slightly sweeter than your target since cold mutes sweetness.
        2. Chill the base. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes before loading.
        3. Load the machine. Pour through the easy-fill port. At 72 oz this is right at max fill. Lock the bail handle and confirm the vessel is sealed.
        4. Select SPIKED SLUSH. Do not use the generic Slush preset.
        5. Run cycle. LEDs pulse while freezing, go solid and beep when ready. Typical cycle is 15–60 min. If not slushing after 60 min, increase temperature one level, wait 10–15 min, repeat as needed.
        6. Serve. Rim glasses with kosher salt, remove cup lid before dispensing, pull handle slowly. Garnish with lime wedges.

        Notes

        • Simple syrup vs agave: simple syrup is 1:1 by volume and dissolves easily cold. Agave is sweeter per ounce — if this version tastes less sweet than an agave batch, add 1–2 tbsp more simple syrup and adjust before loading.
        • Make your own: equal parts sugar and water, heat until dissolved, cool completely. Do NOT add hot syrup to the machine.
        • Low-sugar alert fallback: if the machine triggers a low-sugar alert, stir in 1–2 tbsp simple syrup per 8 oz, reset the SPIKED SLUSH preset, restart.
        • Cointreau vs triple sec: both work at the same volume. Cointreau is drier and cleaner; cheaper triple sec adds more sweetness — adjust simple syrup down slightly if using triple sec.
        ⚠ Scaling preserves ABV automatically — ratios are fixed, not the slider. Always re-taste after rescaling. At 72 oz you're at max fill; reduce batch size if your vessel is smaller.

        Ingredients

          Steps

          1. Mix and taste. Combine pineapple juice, coconut cream, simple syrup, and water. Stir thoroughly — coconut cream separates and needs real mixing. Taste — sweeter than the rum version, since there's no alcohol bite to balance.
          2. Chill the base. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes. Coconut cream blends more evenly cold.
          3. Load the machine. Pour to your scaled max fill line. Lock the bail handle, confirm the seal.
          4. Select SLUSH — not SPIKED SLUSH. The spiked preset assumes alcohol's freeze-point depression, which isn't present here.
          5. Run cycle and monitor. Expect a longer cycle than either rum version. Check at 20 min, then every 10. If it ices too hard, add 1–2 oz more simple syrup and re-run.
          6. Serve. Garnish with a cherry or pineapple wedge. Skip the salted rim.

          Notes

          • Why more sugar, not less: alcohol did real freeze-point work in the rum version. Here sugar is the only lever, so the ratio runs higher to get comparable texture.
          • Use coconut cream, not milk: milk is too thin and may land below the slush threshold even with extra sugar.
          • Texture risk: this batch is most likely to under-perform on texture vs. the alcoholic versions — that's the missing freeze-point depression showing, not an error.
          ⚠ First run = calibration run. Don't reuse rum-recipe timing — this is a different thermal profile.

          Red Slushie — Kids

          Cran-apple based. No alcohol, so sugar alone drives freeze behavior. Use standard SLUSH, not SPIKED SLUSH.

          36 oz
          10.0%

          well above floor

          Sugar Rush-O-Meter™

          Barely SweetCircus ModeGod Help You
          😐

          Ingredients

            Steps

            1. Mix and taste. Combine cran-apple, grenadine, simple syrup, lime juice, and water in a pitcher. Stir well. Taste — tart-sweet, cran-apple dominant, grenadine giving a pomegranate edge. Lime should be present but not sour-forward.
            2. Chill the base. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes before loading.
            3. Load the machine. Pour to your scaled max fill line. Lock the bail handle, confirm the seal.
            4. Select SLUSH — not SPIKED SLUSH. No alcohol means no freeze-point depression from that source.
            5. Run cycle and monitor. Cran-apple is thinner than the piña colada base (no coconut cream body), so this may freeze faster — check at 20 min, then every 10. If too icy, add 1–2 oz simple syrup and re-run.
            6. Serve. Garnish with a cherry or lime wheel.

            Notes

            • Grenadine does double duty: color and sugar. For less sweetness, cut grenadine and add back cran-apple to hold total volume — don't just remove sugar without replacing volume.
            • Lime is the balancer, not the lead: it's there to cut sweetness, not headline the flavor. Adjust a half-ounce at a time.
            • Color note: cran-apple plus grenadine should give deep red without dye. For brighter "kid red," a few drops of food coloring is the only lever — more grenadine just adds sweetness, not color.
            ⚠ Thinner base than the piña colada card — don't reuse its timing assumptions. Check your machine's minimum fill line at smaller batch sizes.
